Appendix B – Usability Testing

Sample Interview Script

Greeting

Thank you for agreeing to participate in this interview regarding the evaluation of the use of Kahoot for Social Studies. Your feedback today will help provide better insight into the use of Kahoot as a tool for learning and understanding in social studies. You will provide feedback to a series of questions, you may discuss other elements of the Kahoot app for learning if they are not discussed, and you will complete a series of tasks relating to the usability of Kahoot (see usability tasks “For Teachers” below).

For Students

Usability Tasks for Users and Kahoot for Social Studies:
  • Note time and number of clicks it took to complete each task.
Questions:
  • Questions about tasks, feelings about Kahoot, etc.

Teachers – Usability Tasks

Please perform as you normally would (normal speed, please read all instructions on the web page) and please ask questions and take breaks as needed.

(For evaluator: document time user takes to complete each task and number of clicks made.)

  1. Task 1: Create a 10-question quiz on a historical topic using the Kahoot Generator.
  2. Task 2: Connect your account to Google Slides and import slides to your Kahoot.
  3. Task 3: Using the Kahoot library, find and open a history-themed slide set.
  4. Task 4: Explore the Kahoot marketplace and locate a Social Studies-themed Kahoot course.
  5. Task 5: Find a Social Studies-themed Kahoot using the Discover page.
Reflection Questions:
  • What was the most challenging aspect of the interface?
  • Did you find it easy to adapt Kahoot features to your specific needs?
  • How well does Kahoot support your assessment of key social studies concepts?
  • How do you integrate Kahoot within broader instructional strategies?
  • Are there any features you wish Kahoot had to support your pedagogical goals?

Students – Usability Tasks

Goals for Student Testing:

View Example Kahoot Quiz

  1. Task 1: Using the Discover tab, find the Social Studies by Kahoot! Channel.
  2. Task 2: Find and open the “Why Do We Learn History” quiz in the Social Studies by Kahoot! Channel.
  3. Task 3: Complete the 10-question “Why Do We Learn History?” quiz.
  4. Task 4: Find and practice using the Flashcard feature.
  5. Task 5: Use the Play Solo feature to review all questions.
Retrospective Think-Aloud Questions:
  • Do you feel like Kahoot helps you remember information better than books or lessons? Explain.
  • Is Kahoot’s pace too fast or too slow? Would you change it?
  • Do you rush to answer or read carefully? Explain.
  • Which tools were most beneficial for your learning?
  • What tools would you use again in school to help retain information?
